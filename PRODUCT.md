# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Aviation maintenance technicians, quality inspectors, group leaders, managers, and directors at an MRO (Maintenance, Repair & Overhaul) organisation. Users work in a high-stakes, compliance-driven environment — errors have safety consequences. They operate across a role hierarchy (Staff → Group Leader → Manager → Director/Admin) and interact with the tool during active maintenance shifts as well as in planning and review contexts.

## Product Purpose

SQD-APP is an internal aviation QA/QC platform for managing audit templates, task assignment, inspections, findings, and work packages. It exists to enforce compliance workflows, maintain an auditable record of quality events, and surface issues before they become safety incidents. Success means inspectors can complete their work without friction and managers can trust the data they see.

## Brand Personality

Serious · Bold · Military-grade — without tipping into ops-room theatrics. The tool carries weight and authority because the domain demands it, not for aesthetic effect. Confidence over decoration; structure over expressiveness.

## Anti-references

- **Consumer SaaS (Notion, Linear, Figma):** No playful pastels, soft rounded blobs, or whitespace-as-personality. This is not a productivity tool for knowledge workers.
- **Military ops UI (dark terminals, amber radar, glowing readouts):** Dark-mode-by-default, glowing accent colours, and HUD aesthetics are a costume, not a system. Avoid.

## Design Principles

1. **Structure signals safety.** Visual hierarchy must make the most critical information — overdue tasks, open findings, escalations — impossible to miss. Hierarchy is functional, not decorative.
2. **Authority through restraint.** Bold weight and deliberate spacing communicate seriousness. Decoration undermines credibility in a compliance tool.
3. **Role clarity at a glance.** The interface adapts to the signed-in role. A staff member's view and a director's view should feel tuned to their job, not identical with sections hidden.
4. **Data density with room to breathe.** MRO users scan lists quickly. Tight rows and clear typographic rhythm serve them better than padded cards. Never sacrifice readability for whitespace elegance.
5. **Zero ambiguity on status.** Every task, finding, and work package has a status. That status must be readable in under one second — colour, label, and icon all aligned.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target. Minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Full keyboard navigation. Screen reader support for data tables and status changes. Reduced-motion alternative for all transitions.
